Welcome to Taniti

Discover a small Pacific island with sandy and rocky beaches, lush rainforests, and a scenic active volcano. Taniti’s coastal bay and walkable city make it easy to relax, explore, and plan the perfect getaway.

General Information

Good to Know

  • Power: 120V (same as U.S.).
  • Currency: USD (many accept euros/yen; cards widely accepted).
  • Alcohol sales: not from midnight–9:00 a.m.
  • Drinking age: 18 (not strictly enforced).
  • Language: English common in tourist areas.
  • Health: 1 hospital + several clinics.
  • Safety: violent crime rare; watch for petty theft.
  • Holidays: many closures on national holidays.

Getting Here & Around

Arrive by air (airport handles jets and prop planes; expansion underway) or by cruise (weekly docking at Yellow Leaf Bay). In town: buses (5 a.m.–11 p.m.), taxis, local car rentals, bikes (helmets required), and easy walking—especially around Merriton Landing.

Things To Do

Beaches, rainforest hikes, volcano visits, museum, fishing charters, snorkeling, zip-lining, pubs and microbrewery, dance club, movies, arcade, galleries, bowling, and a nine-hole golf course (opening soon).

Stay & Eat

Lodging ranges from a hostel to a four-star resort, plus family-owned hotels and B&Bs (all regulated and inspected). Dining: five fish & rice, three American-style, two Pan-Asian. Groceries: two supermarkets, two smaller stores, and a 24-hour convenience store.
