Entertainment & Transportation

Find details about getting around, where to stay, what to eat, and all the activities Taniti offers.

Tourist Needs

This page covers the essentials for visitors: restaurants & grocery, lodging, entertainment & sightseeing, plus transportation and ground transport.

Transportation

Arriving to Taniti

Almost all visitors arrive to Taniti by air, though a small cruise ship docks in Yellow Leaf Bay one night per week. Taniti is served by a small airport that can accommodate small jets and propeller planes. The airport is being expanded so larger jets will be able to land within the next few years.

Getting Around (Ground Transportation)

  • Public buses serve Taniti City daily from 5 a.m. to 11 p.m.
  • Private buses serve the rest of the island.
  • Taxis are available in Taniti City.
  • Rental cars can be rented from a local agency near the airport.
  • Bikes & helmets are available from several vendors (helmets are required by law).
  • Walkability: Taniti City is fairly flat and very walkable. Many tourists stay around Merriton Landing, which is easy to explore on foot.

Entertainment

Most people visit Taniti to enjoy the beaches, explore the rainforest, and visit the volcano. There’s also a wide variety of other activities, many located in Merriton Landing on the north side of Yellow Leaf Bay.

Beaches & Water

  • Relax on white, sandy beaches that encircle Yellow Leaf Bay.
  • Swimming, snorkeling, and chartered fishing tours.
  • Helicopter rides with coastal views.

Rainforest & Volcano

  • Hikes in lush rainforest trails.
  • Zip-lining through the canopy.
  • Visits to Taniti’s active volcano.

Museums, Nightlife & Fun

  • Local history museum.
  • Several pubs, including a microbrewery.
  • New dance club.
  • Movie theater & arcade.
  • Art galleries & bowling.
  • Coming soon: nine-hole golf course (expected operational next year).

Many attractions are clustered in Merriton Landing, a rapidly developing area on Yellow Leaf Bay’s north side.

Sightseeing

Most tourists spend much of their time in Taniti City, known for its native architecture and nearby white beaches that encircle Yellow Leaf Bay. Popular activities include boat or bus tours of the island, hikes in the rainforest, and visits to the active volcano.

Lodging

Taniti offers a wide variety of lodging, ranging from an inexpensive hostel to one large, four-star resort. There are many small, family-owned hotels and a growing number of bed and breakfasts. All types of lodging are strictly regulated and regularly inspected by the Tanitian government.

Restaurants & Grocery

Restaurants

Taniti currently has 10 restaurants:

  • Five serve mostly local fish and rice.
  • Three serve American-style meals.
  • Two serve Pan-Asian cuisine.

Grocery Stores

  • Two supermarkets.
  • Two smaller grocery stores.
  • One convenience store that is open 24 hours a day.
